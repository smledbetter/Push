Acknowledge and normalize negative emotions.
To be fair to the MyFitnessPal team, the push notification above is not bad. It’s polite-ish. But obviously, the user above didn’t think so.
A better strategy is to be real with users. Weigh loss takes years. It's a long, hard, and confusing process. You can help users protect their motivation by hinting that they might feel annoyed, angry, or frustrated on that journey. This rule supports autonomy because it takes the pressure off of users to be pollyanna. It communicates to users that it’s okay if they feel any negative emotions. And because of this, they are more likely to maintain their motivation over the long run. It also takes the pressure off of app designers to act like cheerleaders all the time.
Example: “Hey [Name], we don’t mean to be a Nagging Ned – would you like to update your body weight today? It’s totally cool if you might not feel like it today.”
Limits: This rule won’t work if you tell them how they should feel. Note we didn’t say “you will definitely get annoyed”. We simply suggested it’s possible. We gave them space to decide how they feel. Telling the user how they will feel is subtly coercive. So it pays to use tentative language.
