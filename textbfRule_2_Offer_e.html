Offer empathetic rationales.
An empathetic rationale explains to the user why they should do a thing, given their goals. It explains why any request from the app is personally relevant to them.
Example: “Hey [Name], last time we spoke you mentioned you wanted to track your body weight, this week. Here’s your friendly reminder!”
Limit(s): A limit of this technique is that you have to do the hard work of helping users figure out what they actually want. They might not know what they want, or they might change their mind. But it still on you as the app designer to facilitate that process and to check in regularly to make sure they still want it.
Reference(s):
Steingut, R. R., Patall, E. A., & Trimble, S. S. (2017). The effect of rationale provision on motivation and performance outcomes: A meta-analysis. Motivation Science, 3, 19-50.

Competence

Clarify what realistic progress looks like.
Help them understand what realistic goals and rate of progress is for someone like them.
Example: “Hey [Name], people like you tend to get more out of MyFitnessPal in the long-run when they aim for about 0.5 to 1.0 pound of weight loss every week.”
Limit(s): The user might not believe or want to acknowledge what is realistic for them.
Reference(s):
Hancox, J. E., Quested, E., Thøgersen-Ntoumani, C., & Ntoumanis, N. (2017). The effects of training group exercise class instructors to adopt a motivationally adaptive communication style. Health Psychology and Behavioral Medicine, 3, 190-203.
